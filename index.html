Publishing and digital humanities professional

Summary

I graduated in May with my M.A. in English from the University of Virginia, where I specialize in 19th-century American literature, textual studies, and digital humanities. I participated in this year's Praxis Program in the Scholar's Lab, as well as the Rare Book School UVA Fellowship Program. I am particularly interested in digital applications to textual editing. I am passionate about preserving history, literature, and culture while also making resources for learning about them available to a wider audience. I see digital technology as a way of doing this. I am now seeking work at a publishing house, library, university, or cultural-heritage institution, either in an editorial capacity or as a project manager.

Bio

I grew up in Marietta, Georgia, with my parents and younger sister. I completed my AB in English and Spanish at the University of Georgia in 2010 and moved to Charlotte, where I worked writing closed captioning for television. In the fall of 2012 I entered the Graduate English Program at UVA, and I cannot imagine any place better suited to my interests, both textual and digital. I have taken David Vander Meulen's courses in bibliography and textual editing, and I have delved into DH as an assistant to Alison Booth, an intern at Documents Compass, and a Praxis Fellow.

In my spare time I play the violin, often with a community orchestra, but I enjoy improvisational playing, as well. I also conduct genealogical research on my family when I feel the need for an intense bout of recreational research. On a more casual basis, I have recently picked up dyeing pysanky, Ukrainian Easter eggs. I live with my cat and two roommates in Charlottesville, VA.
