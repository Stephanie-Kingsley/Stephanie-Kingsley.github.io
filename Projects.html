Collective Biographies of Women

Created by University of Virginia professor Alison Booth, Collective Biographies of Women is a digital prosopography (study of biographical networks) which assembles records of women whose lives were chronicled in 19th-century collective biographies. The collection links its subjects by the particular biographies in which they are included. For instance, which other women are included in biographies which include Queen Victoria? By asking and answering such questions, the project examines methods of typing women in the 19th century.

I assisted by editing the person records in the database. I also conducted TEI markup on the biographical texts, another focus of CBW being analyzing the types of narrative elements included in the biographies themselves.

Documents Compass: People of the Founding Era

Documents Compass is a part of the Digital Initiatives of the Virginia Foundation for the Humanities. It maintains the Papers of the Founding Fathers Early Access Project, which provides access to digital editions of the documents written and received by the Founding Fathers. The project People of the Founding Era assembles the persons mentioned in the Founding Fathers' papers in a database, including biographical details such as birth, death, occupation, military service, educational institutions attended, etc.

As an intern in the summer of 2013 for Documents Compass, I expanded the PFE collection to include enslaved people, professors, professor family members, hotel keepers, and students who lived and worked at the University of Virginia.

Praxis Fellowship Program: the Ivanhoe Game WordPress Theme

The Praxis Program is a year-long fellowship which trains humanities graduate students in conceiving, planning, developing, and designing a digital project. Members of the Scholars' Lab guide the students through the entire process of carrying out the project, giving lessons in coding, digital collaboration, project management, and a myriad of other skills necessary to building a DH tool.

I served as project manager on this team, keeping track of deadlines, checking in with team members' progress, making decisions on the scope of the project, and planning publicity. We launched Ivanhoe on April 29. Read about our experiences on our Praxis blog at http://praxis.scholarslab.org/, and learn more about Ivanhoe and download the theme at http://ivanhoe.scholarslab.org/

Collation of M.R. Delany's Blake, or, The Huts of America

For Jerome McGann's Fall 2013 seminar "American Historiography," I am collating the two versions of M.R. Delany's novel Blake, or The Huts of America, one of the earliest African American novels, published in the United States in 1859, then again in 1861, in serial form by the Anglo-African Magazine. No one has collated (comparing word-for-word) the two versions, but such a project is vital in determining whether Delany revised his work. This knowledge could substantially inform our understanding of the mysterious conclusion of the novel. I am using Juxta collation software, by NINES, for the project.

Completed December 2013.

James Fenimore Cooper's Mercedes of Castile: a Digital Edition

For my these, I edited two the Preface and one chapter of Cooper's novel Mercedes of Castile, in which Cooper retells Christopher Columbus's discovery of the Americas. The novel was published in 1840, by Carey and Lea in Philadephia, and then by Richard Bentley in London in 1841. Two other English-language editions came out the same year in Paris, published by A. and W. Galignani and Baudry's European Library. The work was also translated into Spanish, French, and German that year. I have collated (compared word for word) the English-language editions with the manuscript, held in UVA Special Collections, in order to establish what Cooper's final intentions for his work were, and to restore corruptions in the published text.

For my Rare Book School Fellowship, I constructed a digital edition of the portion of Mercedes which I edited. I used XML markup, XSLT, and JavaScript to display the textual witnesses and variants. The edition won Honorable Mention for the Betsy and Stuart Houston Prize for Most Outstanding Project and will be featured on the Rare Book School website [link soon to be posted].

Completed April 2014.
